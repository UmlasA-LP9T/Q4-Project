CHARACTERS IN NOLI ME TANGERE

Padre Dámaso
  • The biological father of Maria Clara is Padre Damaso, a former town priest in San Diego. He attempted to keep his daughter and Crisostomo Ibarra apart for Maria Clara's sake by opposing their marriage.

  • Padre Damaso was a bold and arrogant man. He acted superior to others due to the power that came with becoming a friar. In addition to being horrible, the man's arrogance included foul language, and at Capitan Tiago's celebration, where he was a resident of the Philippines himself, he even went so far as to insult the locals. Despite all of this, he had a soft spot for his daughter and ultimately called off her marriage to Ibarra for her sake.

    • Padre Damaso

Crisostomo Ibarra
  • Crisostomo was raised by his father Don Rafael and was born into the Ibarra family. He and Maria Clara were childhood friends while they were both living in San Diego. He traveled to Europe in 1874 to advance his studies, and while he was gone, Don Rafael and Capitan Tiago engaged him to Maria Clara. After seven years, he eventually came back after learning of his father's passing.

  • Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin, also known as Crisostomo Ibarra, was a young college student from San Diego. After his father passed away, he went back to his hometown to try to start a proper school, but he encountered many challenges from the locals.

    • Crisostomo Ibarra

Basilio
  • Sometime around 1871, Basilio, Sisa's son, was born in San Diego. Along with his younger brother Crispin, they grew up together, and the two later joined the San Diego parish as sacristans.

  • Basilio, Crispin's older brother, was a small child living in San Diego. Basilio left the village after discovering his mother's passing and his brother's abduction, and Capitan Tiago took him in. He moved to Manila and enrolled in medical school while also joining the group of students working to build the Castilian Academy.

    • Basilio

Maria Clara
  • In between Doa Pia and Padre Damaso, Maria Clara was born. Maria Clara was brought up as Capitan Tiago's daughter when her mother passed away after delivery. She and Ibarra were childhood friends, and she was taken to the convent of Sta. Clara. After their parents agreed to betroth them, Ibarra soon departed for Europe.

  • Maria Clara, whose full name is Maria Clara de los Santos, was the fiancée of Crisostomo Ibarra and the daughter of Capitan Tiago. After Ibarra went back to San Diego, Maria Clara encountered a lot of opposition to their engagement.

    • Maria Clara

Padre Salvi
  • When Padre Salvi was appointed San Diego's town curate in the 1880s, he greatly increased his influence among the locals. He also fought the alferez of the Guardia Civil for control of the town at this period. He eventually developed a passion for Maria Clara, Capitan Tiago's stunning daughter. He also learned that Padre Damaso was Maria Clara's biological father around this time when he came upon the priest's letters to Maria Clara's mother, Doa Pia.

  • Padre Salvi succeeded Padre Damaso as the town curate of San Diego. He later planned Crisostomo Ibarra's demise while misleading the locals because of his enmity with the alferez of the town.

    • Padre Salvi

Elias
  • Elias and his twin sister were born into a wealthy household in Manila, where his mother was a wealthy woman's daughter and his father was a prostitute's son. The twins were informed that their mother had passed away while they were little. The two enjoyed good lives while being raised by their maternal grandpa.

  • Elias was a wanted man who resided in San Diego. After seeing tragedy after catastrophe, he started trying to make society better. Crisostomo Ibarra helped the young man and repeatedly saved him after the young man saved his life.

    • Elias

Kapitan Tiago
  • In Malabon, Tiago was born the sole child of a sugar miller. Despite his family's wealth, Tiago's father refused to pay for his education, so a Dominican took him in and sent him to school. Around this period, he tried to court Doa Victorina without success.

  • Maria Clara's father, Capitan Tiago, was a prominent businessman in San Diego. Capitan Tiago strained to follow the friars' instructions and betrothed his daughter to Crisostomo Ibarra.

    • Kapitan Tiago

Don Rafael Ibarra
  • Don Rafael Ibarra, also knows as the father of Cristomo Ibarra. He was the wealthiest guy in San Diego, but he was also the most moral and giving. He therefore trod on the toes of the ruling class, who promptly plotted his demise.

    • Rizal, J. (2006). Noli me tangere. Penguin Classics.

Pilosopo Tasyo o Don Anastacio
  • Tasio, who was born to a wealthy lady, grew into a gifted young man and enrolled in philosophy. But despite his amazing talent, his devout mother urged him to either become a priest or give up his studies. Tasio decided to get married after falling in love with a woman.

  • Anastacio, also known as Pilosopo Tasio, was an elderly scholar who resided in San Diego. He was regarded as a crazy for his unconventional beliefs, yet he ended up being a local advisor for various people.

    • Pilosopo Tasyo o Don Anastacio

Sisa
  • After some time, Sisa moved to San Diego and got married. She gave birth to two kids for her abusive husband, Basilio and Crispin. She also got to know Pilosopo Tasio, her next-door neighbor.

  • Sisa was the mother of Basilio and Crispin and a resident of San Diego. Sisa went wild after both of her boys vanished and wandered the town looking for them.

    • Sisa

Crisipin
  • Crispin, the son of Sisa, and his brother Basilio later worked as sacristans for the church in San Diego. On All Saints' Day in 1881, Crispin and his brother rung the church bells in San Diego. The sacristan mayor had accused Crispin of stealing two gold pieces, and he expressed his concerns to his brother. Crispin was unable to flee while Basilio ran when the sacristan mayor unexpectedly appeared and started thrashing them. Later, he vanished, probably murdered by Padre Salvi and the mayor of Sacristan.

  • Along with his brother Basilio, Crispin served as a sacristan for the church while he was a young kid living in San Diego. Crispin received a punishment from the chief sacristan and Padre Salvi after being suspected of robbing the church of funds.

    • Crispin

Donya Victorina de Espadana
  • Donya Victorina, who was born in 1836, grew up rejecting all of her suitors because she wanted to wed a Spaniard. By 1881, she desperately needed a spouse to maintain her social standing; as a result, she was introduced to the charlatan Don Tiburcio by a common acquaintance. They got hitched shortly after. She started putting on many airs while trying to be a Spaniard at this time, despite being an indigenous person. One of these ruses was claiming to be pregnant and arranging a trip to Spain so she could give birth there.

  • Doa Victorina was a well-known socialite and Capitan Tiago's companion. She and her husband Don Tiburcio aimed to network with San Diego's powerful people in order to elevate their social position.

    • Donya Victorina

Don Tiburcio de Espadaña
  • Doa Victorina's hen-pecked spouse is Don Tiburcio de Espada. Being a proud Spanish doctor in the Philippines was on his wife's agenda. Why did Don Tiburcio become into a quackery physician? Don Tiburcio arrived in the Philippines to work as a small-time customs official.

    • Don Tiburcio de Espadaña

Donya Consolacion
  • Donya Consolacion is a senior Filipina who is the Tenyente's wife. Doa Consolación is a harsh, nasty companion who berates the ensign and picks violent battles with him that are heard throughout the village.

    • Donya Consolacion

Alperes
  • Alperes is the head of the Civil Guard. His wife, Dona Concolacion, is a rival in the power of the priest, so Father Salvi often quarrels with him.

    • Rizal, J. (2006). Noli me tangere. Penguin Classics.

Tiya Isabel
  • Isabel Alba is Kapitan Tiago's cousin as well as Maria Clara's aunt and primary caregiver. In the De Los Santos farm, she serves as the chief housekeeper. Ces Quesada played the role of her.

    • Rizal, J. (2006). Noli me tangere. Penguin Classics.
